About Me

Being a privileged student in Dhaka, I got the chance to use the latest technologies at a very early stage—around 10 or 12 years ago. I always wondered how a computer uses electricity to help me draw things I like and print them.

Later, being able to use Modems and 3G data, I was fascinated: "How do my messages reach my relatives in Australia so fast?"

All these curiosities, along with my affection towards mathematics, led me to ask which degree could take me into the world of computers and communication. The answer was Electrical and Electronics Engineering (EEE)—and it has been one of the best decisions of my life. Today, I am graduating as a Communication Major with knowledge of how a computer runs its programs and how different communication systems work.

The journey from wondering about a message reaching Australia to designing a LAPS with SDR communicating to LEO satellites has been truly fascinating.

"Don't let your success make noise. Let your success be a clean amplified signal" — This philosophy guides my approach to engineering and life.
